## Slide 5: High-Luminosity LHC (HL-LHC) Upgrade

### HL-LHC Technical Specifications
- **Start date**: June 2030 (Run 4)
- **Peak instantaneous luminosity**: 5.0-7.5 × 10³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹
- **Target integrated luminosity**: 3,000 fb⁻¹ by 2041

- **Total data increase**: 10× over LHC design goal

### Key Upgrades
- New superconducting focusing magnets (16 T dipoles)
- Crab cavities for crossing angle correction
- Upgraded detectors (ATLAS/CMS tracker replacement)
- Enhanced radiation protection systems

## Slide 13: Left-Right Symmetric Models (LRSM) - Theoretical Framework

### LRSM Gauge Structure
- **Gauge group**: SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R × U(1)_{B-L}
- **Symmetry restoration**: Left-right parity at high energy
- **Key particles**: 
  - Right-handed W boson (W_R)
  - Heavy right-handed neutrinos (N_R)
  - Additional Z' boson
  - Scalar triplets (Δ_L, Δ_R)

### Physics Motivation
- **Parity restoration**: Explains why weak force violates parity
- **Neutrino masses**: Natural implementation of seesaw mechanism
- **Matter-antimatter asymmetry**: Leptogenesis scenarios
- **Dark matter candidates**: Heavy neutrinos in extended models

## Slide 16: Luminosity Definition and Physics Significance

### Luminosity Definition
**Instantaneous Luminosity (L)**
- L = (Number of events/sec) / Cross-section
- Units: cm⁻²s⁻¹ or barn⁻¹s⁻¹
- **Physical meaning**: Collision rate per unit cross-section

**Integrated Luminosity (L_int)**
- L_int = ∫ L dt
- Units: fb⁻¹ (inverse femtobarns)
- **Physical meaning**: Total data sample size

### Accelerator Performance Comparison
| Collider | L_inst (cm⁻²s⁻¹) | Comments |
|----------|------------------|-----------|
| Tevatron | 4.0×10³² | pp̄ collisions |
| LHC (current) | 2.1×10³⁴ | pp collisions |
| HL-LHC | 5.0×10³⁴ | Design goal |
| LEP | 1.0×10³² | e⁺e⁻ collisions |
